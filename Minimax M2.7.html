1
Normal Blood Flow
Red blood cells, platelets, and white blood cells flow smoothly through the intact blood vessel.
2
Vessel Wall Injury
Endothelial damage exposes subendothelial collagen and von Willebrand factor (vWF).
3
Platelet Adhesion
Platelets bind to exposed collagen and vWF via glycoprotein receptors (GPIb/IX/V).
4
Platelet Activation
Activated platelets change shape and release ADP and thromboxane A2 to recruit more platelets.
5
Coagulation Cascade
The cascade converts prothrombin to thrombin, which converts fibrinogen to fibrin mesh.
6
Stable Thrombus
Fibrin mesh traps platelets and red blood cells, forming a stable clot that restricts blood flow.
7
Clinical Outcomes
The thrombus may dissolve (fibrinolysis), break off as an embolus, or occlude the vessel.
